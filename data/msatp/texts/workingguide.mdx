<h2>Section A</h2>
<p>Systematic Analysis of Artifacts & Ceramics from an Archaeological Site</p>
<ol>
<li>Sort artifacts from excavation squares and units into type of materials (ceramics/pottery, metal, glass, stone etc)</li>
<li>Sort ceramics into types according to material (determined by firing temperature): porcelain, stoneware, and earthenware</li>
<li>Sort the material types into sub-categories:</li>
<ul><li>a. Earthenware:- fine paste and tempered earthenware</li>
<li>b. Stoneware:- buff non-tempered stoneware, tempered brittle stoneware, and Burmese glazed stoneware</li>
<li>c. Porcelain:- green, white, blue and white, and green-splashed etc</li></ul>
<li>Divide into vessel types: storage vessels, cooking pots, libation vessels etc.</li>
<li>Divide into features and body sherds:</li>
<ul><li>vessel parts: rims, bases, lugs etc</li>
<li>vessel types: jars, bowls, vases, cups, etc</li>
<li>function of vessels</li>
<li>size: rims (diameter) and bases</li>
<li>number of vessels (by type)</li>
<li>decoration (motifs and techniques): </li>
<ul><li>chronology (how to date the ceramics?)</li>
<li>inter-site relationship (e.g. similar motifs/symbols/decoration or painting techniques/other types)</li></ul></ul>
